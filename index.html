Safety Constrained Deep Reinforcement Learning Control for Human-Robot Collaboration in Construction

Kangkang Duan1, Zhengbo Zou2
1The University of British Columbia
2Columbia University
2024
Paper

Abstract

Worker safety has become an increasing concern in human-robot collaboration (HRC) due to potential new hazards and risks introduced by robots. Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) has demonstrated to be efficient in training robots to acquire complex construction skills. However, conventional neural network policies for collision avoidance lack theoretical safety guarantees and face challenges with out-of-distribution scenarios. To address this, we propose a biomimetic safety constrained DRL control method inspired by vertebrates' decision-making systems: a neural network functions as the brain, generating reference actions, while a reference governor layer acts as the spinal cord, determining the applied reference actions. This approach enhances DRL by enabling robots to intuitively respond to environmental stimuli, prioritizing safety and ensuring rapid responses. Theoretical safety guarantees related to torque, joint limitations, velocity, and distance were analyzed. Experiment results demonstrate that the proposed method can provide a safe HRC mode in both static and dynamic scenarios for construction.
